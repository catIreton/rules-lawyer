## Objective

Score the **most total points** from:
- Claiming railway routes
- Completing destination tickets
- Longest Continuous Path bonus

---

## Setup

1. Place the board. Each player takes **45 train pieces** and a **scoring marker** at 0
2. Shuffle and deal **4 train cards** to each player
3. Shuffle destination tickets; deal **3 to each player** — keep at least 2, return the rest face down
4. Reveal 5 train cards face-up beside the deck

---

## Turn — Choose One Action

### 1. Draw Train Cards
- Take **2 cards** total from:
  - Any **face-up** cards (flip replacement immediately)
  - The top of the **face-down deck**
- **Locomotive (wild)** face-up cards count as only **1 draw** (you can't draw a second card after taking a face-up loco)

### 2. Claim a Route
- Play the exact number of matching-color cards for that route
- Place your train pieces on every space of the route
- **Gray routes** can be claimed with any one single color
- Score points immediately (see table below)

### 3. Draw Destination Tickets
- Take **3 destination tickets** from the deck
- Keep at least **1**, return the rest face down under the deck

---

## Route Scoring

| Route Length | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 4 |
| 4 | 7 |
| 5 | 10 |
| 6 | 15 |

---

## Destination Tickets

Each ticket shows **two cities** and a point value.

- **Completed** at end of game: **add** the value to your score
- **Incomplete** at end of game: **subtract** the value from your score
- Keep secret until final scoring

---

## End Game Trigger

When any player has **2 or fewer** train pieces left after their turn, **each other player takes one final turn** (including the triggering player who still gets to play that turn normally).

---

## Final Scoring

After the last round:

1. Add or subtract all destination ticket values
2. Award **+10 points** to the player with the **Longest Continuous Path** of connected routes
   - In case of a tie, all tied players earn the bonus
3. Highest total wins; ties broken by most completed destination tickets

---

## Double Routes (3+ players)

On maps with parallel double routes between two cities, each route may only be claimed by **one player**. In a 2-player game, only one of the double routes may be claimed at all.
